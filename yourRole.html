Your Role



What YOU can do about misinformation:

Fake News Hotspots

Social media, magazines, and small new sites are all hotspots for the spread of misinformation! For example, fake news spreads 70% faster than real news does on Twitter.

Recognize Red Flags

- Bad grammar in an article
- Suspicious sites
- Lack of legitimate sources
- Sensational news that seems overly exaggerated, biased, or strange

Fact-check!

Legitimate news sites will have legitimate sources! If only one news site is reporting on a major event, be skeptical. Beware of circular reporting, in which 2 or more fake news sites credit each other to paint the illusion of proper sources.

Consider the Author's Agenda

There are many people who stand to gain something by generating fake news. Politicians who want exposure, small news sites that want more clicks, or scammers that want to incite chaos and fear.
